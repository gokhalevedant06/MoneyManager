PUBLIC PROVIDENT FUND

PPF is long term investment which is risk free and tax free. The interest rate is an attractive 8 % PA and the invested amount can be claimed under Section 80C to lower your tax liability. The lock-in period is 15 Years, after which you can choose to withdraw your corpus. The minimum amount that needs to be deposited is just Rs 500 and the maximum amount is capped at Rs 1.5 Lakh annually. A very interesting fact about PPF money saving scheme that many investors are unaware of is that you can take a loan against the amount you invested in PPF.

Major Benefits

  • 1) The major reason why PPF is considered as a preferred choice can be attributed to it being a safe investment option.
  • 2) Not only this, the interest earned on invested amount is also exempt from tax.
  • 3) As a saving scheme by the government, PPF gives an agreeable rate of interest and returns on investments.
  • 4) You can open a post office PPF account either by cheque or cash, whichever is more convenient.

Some Causions

  • 1) The lock period is 15 years.
  • 2) You cant withdraw money before 15 years.
