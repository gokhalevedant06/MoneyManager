FIXED DEPOSIT

A fixed deposit (FD) is a financial instrument provided by banks or NBFCs which provides investors a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account, until the given maturity date. FD is basically a loan given to bank by us on which bank provide interest on it. FD can be for maximum 20 years.

Types of FDs

  • Regular FD : In this type the persons above 60 years will get regilar interest.

  • Flexi FD : when our saving account is greater than threshhold valuethen bank convert it to FD and when balance get less then                 money is transferred to saving this gives high opportunities to gain Intrest.

  • Tax saving FD : Tax Saving fixed deposits are suitable for investors looking for lower risk and a fixed and guaranteed return                           on a long- term basis. Deposit made is allowed as a deduction under Section 80C up to Rs 1.5 lakh. If you                           invest say Rs 50000, and your total income is taxed in the 20% tax slab rate, a deduction of Rs 10000 (Rs                           50000 * 20%) will be allowed.

  • Seniors FD : Senior citizens get 0.5% or 1% more interst than for normal persons.

Major Benefits

  • 1) One of the major advantages of opening an FD account in the post office is that TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is not deducted on the interest earned.
  • 2) Like bank FDs, investors earn a guaranteed return through the tenure of the post office term deposit. ... For one-year time deposit to three years, it offers an interest rate of 5.5%. For five year time deposit account, Post Office offers an interest rate of 6.7%.
  • 3) Customers can avail loans against FDs up to 80 to 90 percent of the value of deposits. The rate of interest on the loan could be 1 to 2 percent over the rate offered on the deposit.
  • 4) Investing in a fixed deposit earns customers a higher interest rate than depositing money in a saving account.
